For an Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR) session involving a moving dot, the ideal background and foreground colors should prioritize calm, minimal visual strain, and consistent tracking. The primary goal is to avoid overstimulating the nervous system while facilitating a clear, hypnotic movement.


Ideal Background Colors
The background should be grounding, dark, or neutral to reduce eye strain and help the client focus solely on the movement. 

Dark Grey or Black: This is the most recommended background. It minimizes screen glare and allows the foreground dot to stand out without causing visual fatigue over time.
Deep Blue: Associated with calmness, trust, and safety, a deep blue background can help anxious clients feel more at ease.
Muted Green or Sage: Promotes a sense of balance, natural flow, and reduces mental fatigue.
Dark Purple: Useful for fostering inner strength and a meditative state. 


Ideal Dot (Foreground) Colors 
The dot should be easily tracked, vibrant enough to be seen, but not so bright that it causes sharp contrast strain. 

White or Light Yellow: These provide high contrast against a dark background, making them easy to track without being harsh.
Soft Green/Sage: Creates a gentle visual experience that is soothing to the eye.
Orchid or Light Blue: Other soft, calming options that contrast well with dark backgrounds.
Soft Gold/Yellow: Can feel uplifting and motivating if a more positive energy is needed. 
Recommended Color Combinations
High Contrast/Low Strain (Best Practice): Dark Gray/Black Background + White Dot.
Calming/Soothing: Navy Blue Background + Light Yellow/White Dot.
Nature-Based: Dark Green Background + Soft Green/White Dot. 

Considerations for Color Selection
Avoid High-Saturation Neon: Neon or extremely bright colors can act as irritants and raise physiological arousal, making it harder for the client to relax.
Avoid Sharp Black-and-White: While high contrast is good, a stark black-and-white pattern can create unnecessary strain on the eyes.
Customize to Client: Some clients may need more energizing colors (yellows) if they are feeling low, while others need calming colors (blues, greens).
Visual Check: Always confirm that the contrast allows for a clear visual target that does not blend into the background.
